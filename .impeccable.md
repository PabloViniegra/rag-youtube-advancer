## Design Context

### Users
Bilingual (Spanish/English) YouTube creators — primarily LATAM and Spain — who are growth-focused and monetization-aware. They consume English-language tools and creator resources but produce Spanish content. Mid-tier: past the "hobby" phase, trying to professionalize. They're tech-forward, compare themselves to top creators, and feel urgency about growth. They use tools like TubeBuddy, VidIQ, and Notion. Context: late nights, laptop, headphones, checking analytics obsessively.

**Job-to-be-done**: Extract more money and growth out of every video they publish. Stop leaving views and RPM on the table.

### Brand Personality
**Three words: Bold. Precise. Relentless.**

The brand is a co-pilot for serious creators — not a toy, not a corporate dashboard. It has startup energy and conviction. It talks like a high-performance coach, not a SaaS product. It's the thing you use when you're serious about making YouTube your actual career.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Energetic, growth-obsessed, confident. Think: high-performance sports + editorial media, not AI startup template.
- **Anti-references**: NOT dark mode + cyan/purple neon glows + gradient text (current state). NOT gaming/Discord RGB aesthetic. NOT sterile corporate SaaS.
- **Positive references**: The energy of a Bloomberg terminal meets the personality of a creator economy publication. Data-forward but with human conviction.
- **Theme**: Can evolve beyond the current dark-only approach. Consider a bold light mode or a strong two-tone editorial system.
- **Typography**: Needs a more distinctive display font. Inter as body is overused — needs to feel more deliberate.
- **Color**: The current Red/Purple/Cyan on dark is textbook AI slop palette. Should evolve toward a more intentional, unexpected palette that still carries energy. Consider editorial reds with off-white backgrounds, or a deep green + amber system, or deep navy with high-contrast cream.

### Design Principles
1. **Every pixel earns its place** — no decorative blurs, no gradient overlays just to look "techy". Visual elements must communicate something.
2. **Data as the hero** — if there are numbers, make them sing. Metrics and insights are the product's core value; present them with confidence.
3. **Creator energy, not AI energy** — the interface should feel like it was made for YouTubers who mean business, not for a Y Combinator pitch deck.
4. **Bilingual-native** — no half-measures on language. Either full Spanish or a deliberate bilingual system. Don't mix casually.
5. **Actions over aesthetics** — the primary action (analyze a video) should be unmissable. Every section should funnel toward it.
